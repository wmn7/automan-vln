Overview

Learning human-like object search in a unified BEV decision space.

ObjectNav asks a robot to find an unseen target in an unknown home under partial observability. The core difficulty is deciding where to explore next over a long horizon — long before the target is ever observed.

Why

VLM policies lose either space or visual detail

First-person RGB policies keep appearance but discard spatial structure and act reactively over short horizons; text map summaries keep layout but flatten geometry and fine-grained visual cues. Both drift into repetitive search — turning in place, backtracking, and revisiting the same rooms.

How

Recover search intent from human demonstrations

Frontier-based Human-Intent Labeling looks ahead in low-level human trajectories and labels the frontier that best explains the demonstrator's future search direction — turning raw human actions into high-level BEV waypoint supervision, with no oracle shortest paths.

What

BEV-grounded spatial-visual candidate policy

A VLM compares frontier and target candidates in one BEV decision space, each grounded by its egocentric visual memory and agent-centric geometry, then emits a reserved candidate-ID waypoint that transfers across embodiments.

Key Contributions

Three ideas that make search human-like and transferable.

1

Frontier-based Human-Intent Labeling

We replay Habitat-Web human ObjectNav demonstrations and convert their low-level actions into candidate-level waypoint labels. When a target is verified we supervise it directly; otherwise we infer intent from how frontiers evolve, labeling the frontier the demonstrator actually committed to.

  • 23,767 human episodes → 2.36M candidate-level samples
  • No oracle shortest-path supervision
2

BEV-Grounded Spatial-Visual Policy

Exploration and target commitment are unified as selection over BEV candidates. Each candidate carries its first-observed egocentric visual memory and an agent-centric geometry feature, and an Intent-Aligned Objective smooths frontier choices by direction while keeping hard commitment to verified targets.

  • 32 reserved candidate-ID tokens — selection, not free-form text
  • Angular soft target for directionally consistent search
3

State-of-the-Art & Zero-Shot Transfer

Trained only on MP3D demonstrations, IntentNav reaches the best SR and SPL among learning-based methods on MP3D, HM3D-v1, and HM3D-v2. The same checkpoint and candidate-level BEV interface transfer with no VLM fine-tuning across three physical robots.

  • 82.2% SR on HM3D-v2 • 70.5% on HM3D-v1
  • Wheeled, Go2 quadruped, G1 humanoid • ~0.8s / decision
